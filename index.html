Fender Telecaster

Arguably, the best guitar to ever exist

Bold, innovative and rugged, the Telecaster is pure Fender, through and through. The feel, the style and, most importantly, the sound - they're all there, waiting for you to make them whisper or wail for your music. Versatile enough to handle almost anything you can create and durable enough to survive any gig, this workhorse is a trusty sidekick for your musical vision.

Here's a brief history of the Fender Telecaster:

  • 1949: Introduced by Fender as the Broadcaster, later renamed Telecaster due to trademark issues.
  • 1950: The first production models of the Telecaster became available for purchase.
  • 1951: The Telecaster gained popularity among country and western musicians due to its bright, twangy tone.
  • 1952: Fender introduced the single-cutaway design, which became a signature feature of the Telecaster.
  • 1954: Fender introduced the Telecaster Custom, featuring a body with binding and sunburst finishes.
  • 1958: Fender introduced the Telecaster Thinline, featuring a semi-hollow body for reduced weight and enhanced resonance.
  • 1968: Fender introduced the Telecaster Deluxe, featuring dual humbucking pickups for a thicker, more powerful tone.
  • 1972: Fender introduced the Telecaster Deluxe with Wide Range humbucking pickups, designed by Seth Lover.
  • 1982: Fender introduced the Telecaster Elite, featuring active electronics and innovative features such as a locking tremolo system.
  • 1998: Fender introduced the American Series Telecaster, featuring updated hardware, pickups, and finishes.
  • 2008: Fender celebrated the 60th anniversary of the Telecaster with special commemorative models and events.
  • Present: The Telecaster remains one of the most iconic and enduring electric guitar models, with numerous variations and reissues produced by Fender and other manufacturers.
